Survival Guide

Tap a topic to expand our best advice.

  • Stay organized and lean on your cohort!
  • Try to get the easy assignments done and out of the way by the first 2-3 weeks. Saves you time at the end. Show up — do YOUR best. You got this.
  • Take one day at a time. Stay organized and on top of your work. Make sure you take time to enjoy yourself!
  • Make sure to take time off before beginning.
  • The faculty is there to help you so use them if you are struggling. Keep reviewing pharmacology even when the anesthesia pharm class is complete first semester.
  • Work together as a team, help each other.
  • Take it day by day. The days are long but the weeks are so fast.
  • Start forcing yourself to set a monthly budget early. Summer is jam-packed with multiple tests a week — learn how to set up a good schedule and not cram every week. It is okay to struggle and not get A+'s on everything... the competition is over, we are all in this together. Lean on your friends!
  • You're stuck with these people for 3 years — make a point to reach out and talk/learn/ask questions of everyone!
  • Be patient with the slow processes of Hunter College, everything will work out (after a LOT of stress). Help each other! Your cohort's collaboration will be key to success.
  • Develop good time management because balancing clinical and studying can be difficult. Be flexible — in this program nothing is set in stone, everything is always changing.
  • Focus on active recall and spaced repetition over re-reading notes. Form small study groups to talk through complex concepts like hemodynamics and pharmacology, and prioritize understanding the "why" rather than just memorizing facts.
  • 📖Read: Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning — the research-backed playbook for retaining dense material. Required reading before your first exam season.
  • 🃏Learn Anki (or a similar spaced-repetition platform) — set it up before day one. Use it daily. Your future self will be very, very grateful.
  • ✅ Apex Anesthesia Review — paid for by Hunter, use it religiously. This is your primary question bank and review platform.

  • Most textbooks on the syllabus are available as PDFs — check before you buy. Pick one to supplement lectures and stick with it:

    • Nagelhout & Elisha (Nurse Anesthesia)— written specifically for CRNAs, approachable, and well-organized for how this program teaches.
    • Morgan & Mikhail— dense but thorough; great for mechanism-heavy learners who want to understand the "why" behind every drug and technique.
    • Barash (Clinical Anesthesia)— the gold standard reference. Better as a deep-dive supplement than a cover-to-cover read; reach for it when a topic needs more depth.
    • Miller's Anesthesia— the encyclopedia. Brilliant for specific rabbit holes but way too much for day-to-day studying. Save it for when you need the authoritative answer on one topic.
    • Jaffe's Anesthesiologist's Manual of Surgical Procedures— the go-to for case planning. Essential when you need to understand the surgical procedure, positioning, and anesthetic considerations before stepping into the OR.
  • 💡 Whatever you choose — one textbook done well beats four textbooks skimmed. Consistency matters more than having every resource.

Fuel & Food

Cohort-approved coffee spots in the Financial District.

☕ Coffee & Caffeine

Bluestone Lane

Aussie vibes

30 Broad St & multiple FiDi locations. Excellent flat whites, avocado toast, and a calm atmosphere for pre-shift prep or post-shift decompression.

Gregory's Coffee

NYC staple

100 William St. A NYC-born chain that takes its espresso seriously — strong, fast, and reliable when you have 10 minutes before rounds.

Joe Coffee

Local legend

125 Maiden Lane. A beloved NYC independent with excellent single-origin options and a cozy spot to crack open Nagelhout between shifts.

Cultura Coffee

Hidden gem

90 John St. A quieter, less-crowded option with great espresso and a relaxed vibe — perfect for squeezing in an Anki session before heading in.

Brookfield Place Food Hall

Bonus spot

230 Vesey St. Not just coffee — multiple vendors including Le District for a proper café au lait and pastry. Great for a longer study break or grabbing a real meal between a morning and afternoon case.
