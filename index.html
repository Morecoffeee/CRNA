Your toolkit 🛠️

Study Tools

Tap a topic to expand our best advice.

  • Stay organized and lean on your cohort!
  • Try to get the easy assignments done and out of the way by the first 2-3 weeks. Saves you time at the end. Show up — do YOUR best. You got this.
  • Take one day at a time. Stay organized and on top of your work. Make sure you take time to enjoy yourself!
  • Make sure to take time off before beginning.
  • The faculty is there to help you so use them if you are struggling. Keep reviewing pharmacology even when the anesthesia pharm class is complete first semester.
  • Work together as a team, help each other.
  • Take it day by day. The days are long but the weeks are so fast.
  • Start forcing yourself to set a monthly budget early. Summer is jam-packed with multiple tests a week — learn how to set up a good schedule and not cram every week. It is okay to struggle and not get A+'s on everything... the competition is over, we are all in this together. Lean on your friends!
  • You're stuck with these people for 3 years — make a point to reach out and talk/learn/ask questions of everyone!
  • Be patient with the slow processes of Hunter College, everything will work out (after a LOT of stress). Help each other! Your cohort's collaboration will be key to success.
  • Develop good time management because balancing clinical and studying can be difficult. Be flexible — in this program nothing is set in stone, everything is always changing.
🧠

How to Study

  • Focus on active recall and spaced repetition over re-reading notes. Form small study groups to talk through complex concepts like hemodynamics and pharmacology, and prioritize understanding the "why" rather than just memorizing facts.
  • 📖Read: Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning — the research-backed playbook for retaining dense material. Required reading before your first exam season.
  • 🃏Learn Anki (or a similar spaced-repetition platform) — set it up before day one. Use it daily. Your future self will be very, very grateful.
📱

Apps

  • 📝 Notability — the go-to note-taking app for iPad. Record lectures while you annotate slides, then replay audio synced to your handwriting. Worth every penny for CRNA school.
  • 🗂️ Notion — all-in-one workspace for organizing notes, case logs, schedules, and study plans. Great for keeping everything in one place across your phone, tablet, and laptop.
  • 💊 Vargo App — anesthesia drug reference at your fingertips. Perfect for quick lookups on dosing, mechanisms, and clinical pearls before and during cases.
▶️

YouTube & Video

  • 🥷 Ninja Nerd — free YouTube channel with incredibly clear, visual explanations of physiology and pharmacology. A lifesaver when lectures leave you confused. Watch before class, not after.
🎙️

Podcasts

  • 🎧 Core Anesthesia Podcast — high-yield anesthesia concepts broken down into digestible episodes. Great for commutes, the gym, or winding down.
  • ⚡ Atomic Anesthesia — rapid-fire, concise anesthesia pearls in short episodes. Perfect when you only have 10-15 minutes.
✅

Apex Anesthesia Review

Paid for by Hunter — use it religiously. Your primary question bank and review platform.

📖 Pick one textbook to supplement lectures. Most are available as PDFs — check before you buy:

Nagelhout & Elisha — Nurse Anesthesia

Written specifically for CRNAs, approachable, and well-organized for how this program teaches.

Morgan & Mikhail — Clinical Anesthesiology

Dense but thorough. Great for learners who want to understand the "why" behind every drug and technique.

Barash — Clinical Anesthesia

Gold standard reference. Better as a deep-dive supplement than cover-to-cover — reach for it when a topic needs more depth.

Miller's Anesthesia

The encyclopedia. Best for specific deep dives — too much for daily studying. Save it for when you need the definitive answer.

Jaffe's — Anesthesiologist's Manual of Surgical Procedures

Go-to for case planning. Essential for understanding the surgical procedure, positioning, and anesthetic considerations before stepping into the OR.

💡

One textbook done well beats four textbooks skimmed. Pick one and stick with it.

Loan Types to Know

Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loans

The most common option. Interest accrues while you're in school but fixed rates are manageable. Apply via FAFSA.

Graduate PLUS Loans

RIP 🪦. Unfortunately, The big beautiful bill took this away from us.

HRSA Nursing Scholarships

Federal scholarship for nursing students who commit to working in underserved areas after graduation. Worth applying — competitive but real money.

Private / Hospital Loans

Some hospitals offer tuition assistance or loan repayment in exchange for a work commitment post-graduation. Worth asking your clinical sites.

💡

Budgeting Tips from Cohort 1

  • Set a monthly budget before you stop working — it's a shock to the system and harder to adjust once you're in the thick of school.
  • Track every expense for the first month. Most people are surprised where money actually goes once they have no income.
  • Look into PRN / per diem nursing shifts if your schedule allows in later semesters — some cohort members picked up occasional shifts to offset costs.
  • File your FAFSA as early as October 1st for the upcoming academic year. Earlier = more options.
